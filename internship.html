MECHANICAL TESTING AND FEA SIMULATION OF A RUBBER ENGINE MOUNT

This is an internship that I completed at G.B Rubber Products, during my undergraduate studies.

As part of my undergraduate studies in Chemical Engineering, I had the opportunity to intern at G.B Rubber Products, a company specializing in rubber components for the automotive industry. The goal was to explore the full lifecycle of a rubber engine mount—critical for minimizing engine vibrations and improving ride quality.

I was tasked with simulating the behavior of rubber using ABAQUS/CAE, applying the Ogden hyperelastic model to analyze stress-strain behavior. Using real-world test data from equipment like the Tensometer, I validated the simulations against experimental results. Alongside this, I gained hands-on experience in the manufacturing process—supporting in rubber compounding, vulcanization, and bonding techniques like injection and compression molding.

To ensure material integrity and consistency, I used tools like the Moving Die Rheometer and Video Measuring System to evaluate properties such as cure behavior, hardness, and dimensional precision. This integrated approach—linking simulation, processing, and material testing—provided a comprehensive understanding of how formulation, processing conditions, and mechanical characterization influence the performance of elastomeric materials.
