PROCESS ENGINEERING INTERNSHIP

During my internship at G.B. Rubber Products, I gained hands-on exposure to the manufacturing and processing of elastomer-based components used in automotive applications. My work focused on developing a process-level understanding of rubber component production, from material preparation to final product inspection.

I studied the complete manufacturing workflow and observed how equipment and process parameters such as temperature, pressure, and cycle time influence part consistency, and defect formation on the production floor. In parallel, I worked on finite element simulation of a rubber component using ABAQUS to study failure analysis of the component. Simulation inputs were generated using experimental test data, and the simulation results were compared against physical testing to improve confidence in the predictive behavior of the model.

Alongside this, I gained exposure to production quality and inspection techniques, including physical and mechanical testing, as well as evaluation of material properties. Overall, this internship provided a strong foundation in manufacturing processes, simulation-based analysis, and quality inspection, with skills applicable to process engineering roles across manufacturing and materials-focused industries.
