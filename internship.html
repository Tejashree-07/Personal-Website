MECHANICAL TESTING AND FEA SIMULATION OF A RUBBER ENGINE MOUNT

This is an internship that I completed at G.B Rubber Products, during my undergraduate studies.

This project focuses on the design, manufacturing, and simulation of a rubber engine mount, an important part of vehicles that helps reduce engine vibrations. Engine mounts are critical for improving ride quality and ensuring the engine stays securely in place, so their design and performance must meet high standards. Using ABAQUS/CAE for finite element analysis (FEA), the project studied how rubber behaves under different conditions like stress, strain, and load. The Ogden hyperelastic model was used to simulate the material's performance, and the results were compared with experimental data to ensure accuracy.

The manufacturing process involved rubber compounding, vulcanization, and adhesive bonding, with techniques like injection molding, compression molding, and transfer molding used to shape the engine mount. Post-molding methods such as air de-flashing, cryogenic de-flashing, and tumbling ensured a clean and precise finish. Advanced equipment like the Moving Die Rheometer and Tensometer tested material properties, while tools such as the Video Measuring System (VMS) and Durometer maintained strict quality control. These steps ensured the final product met industry standards for durability and performance.

By combining simulations with real-world testing, the project provided a comprehensive understanding of the end-to-end process involved in designing and manufacturing engine mounts. It highlighted the importance of material properties, precise manufacturing techniques, and quality control in delivering reliable and efficient automotive components.
