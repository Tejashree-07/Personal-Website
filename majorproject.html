MODELING AND OPTIMIZATION OF PROCESS SYSTEMS

I led a three-member team on a process engineering project focused on modeling, analyzing, and optimizing a complex multi-unit manufacturing system handling a mixed material stream under tightly controlled operating conditions

The goal was to improve product quality and process efficiency while reducing energy demand, mirroring challenges common in high-volume and high-precision manufacturing environments. The system consisted of multiple interconnected unit operations, each with distinct temperature, pressure, and flow constraints, requiring coordinated design and optimization.

To achieve this objective, a detailed steady-state process model in Aspen HYSYS was developed. Material and energy balances were used to verify process consistency and identify bottlenecks and inefficiencies within the system. To improve performance, key operating parameters of the process and equipment were varied, to evaluate their impact on throughput, energy consumption, and equipment sizing. These studies enabled identification of robust operating windows that balanced performance targets with practical design constraints.

This project strengthened my skills in process modeling, parameter optimization, and data-driven decision-making, where tightly coupled process steps, energy efficiency, and yield optimization are critical.
