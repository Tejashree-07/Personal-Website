SIMULATION AND OPTIMIZATION OF NGL FRACTIONATION

I worked as part of a team of three on a project centered on optimizing the fractionation process of natural gas to extract valuable components such as methane, ethane, and propane.

The objective was to simulate and optimize the distillation process to maximize product purity while minimizing energy consumption and column size. Designing and refining key distillation columns—De-methanizer, De-ethanizer, and De-propanizer—through both theoretical analysis and simulation was carried out.

Using ASPEN HYSYS, a detailed process model was developed that included thermodynamic property selection, feed characterization, and unit operations like distillation columns, condensers, and reboilers. The McCabe-Thiele method was applied to calculate theoretical tray numbers and material and energy balances were perfromed to validate system feasibility. Further, multiple scenarios were explored by varying reflux ratios and stage numbers to achieve optimal separation. This optimization ensured better separation efficiency with lower energy costs.
