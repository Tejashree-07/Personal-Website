SIMULATION AND OPTIMIZATION OF NGL FRACTIONATION

I worked as part of a team of three on a project centered on optimizing the fractionation process of natural gas to extract valuable components such as methane, ethane, and propane. The project aimed to improve efficiency and purity through a detailed analysis and simulation of the process. We utilized ASPEN HYSYS extensively to model the system, simulate different scenarios, and analyze the impact of various parameters such as reflux ratio and stage numbers on the overall separation efficiency.

The project involved conducting material and energy balances to ensure accurate input-output relationships across all unit operations. Using the McCabe-Thiele methodology, we calculated the theoretical number of trays required for the separation columns. Each distillation column was carefully designed with specified operating conditions, including pressure, temperature, and flow rates, tailored to the characteristics of the natural gas components being processed.

In addition to designing the columns, we explored optimization techniques to improve the process. By adjusting variables such as reflux ratios and column stages, we achieved better separation efficiency with lower energy costs. These refinements were crucial for meeting the desired product specifications while ensuring that the process remained economically viable.
