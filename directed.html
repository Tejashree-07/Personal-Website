Manufacturable Process Design for High-Performance Thermal Interface Materials

This project focused on designing a scalable manufacturing process for high-performance thermal interface materials used in electronics and other high-heat systems. Rather than treating material development as a purely materials-science problem, the work approached it as a process optimization and integration challenge, with emphasis on controllability, stability, and manufacturability.

Material selection was guided by chemical compatibility, mechanical compliance, and process feasibility, ensuring that the system could be manufactured using cost-effective, industry-relevant equipment. The process was intentionally designed to minimize complexity while maintaining the ability to consistently produce high-performance material.

To ensure scalability and predictability, computational fluid dynamics (CFD) was used to analyze flow behavior within the equipment nozzle, evaluating how key parameters such as shear stress, velocity, and viscosity influence flow stability and defect risk in non-Newtonian composite systems. This simulation-driven approach enabled early identification of process bottlenecks, including clogging risk and unstable flow regimes, prior to physical manufacturing.

A Design of Experiments (DOE) framework was then applied to systematically evaluate critical process parameters and quantify trade-offs between material performance and process robustness. This allowed operating conditions to be optimized with manufacturability in mind, reducing sensitivity to variation and lowering the risk of defects in the product to be manufactured.

The outcome of this work was a stable operating window that balances performance targets with repeatable, manufacturable process conditions. Overall, the project demonstrates a process-driven approach to material performance, highlighting how simulation, DOE, and rheology-informed decision-making can be used to:

  • Reduce process variability
  • Identify and mitigate manufacturing risks early
  • Establish stable, repeatable operating conditions
