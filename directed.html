MICRO-SCALE THERMAL MANAGEMENT IN SEMICONDUCTOR DEVICES USING ADDITIVE MANUFACTURING

As a part of my MS course I'm currently undertaking a directed research under Dr Yong Chen. In response to the increasing demand for efficient thermal management in compact and high-performance semiconductor devices, my research focuses on developing micro-scale thermal management solutions. to optimize heat-management solutions in semiconductor chips, using additive manufacturing techniques.

I am currently focusing on conducting thermal simulations using COMSOL to predict heat transfer characteristics in micro-scale heat-sinks. These simulations involve -

Material-Specific Analysis: Modeling heat dissipation properties of graphene and CuCuZr alloy, materials compatible with EHD-Jet Prinitng and Selective Laser Melting (SLM).

Design Optimization: Evaluating the impact of design parameters such as fin structure, surface area, and material conductivity to optimize heat sink performance

In the upcoming months of Spring 25 semester, I plan to use AUTOCAD to create detailed 3D designs of heat sink and fabricate the heat sink prototype using the most appropriate additive manufacturing method. If possible I also aim to conduct thermal experiments to validate the simulation results.

am always open to new ideas, insights, and potential collaborations to expand the scope and impact of this work.
