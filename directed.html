MICRO-SCALE THERMAL MANAGEMENT IN SEMICONDUCTOR DEVICES USING ADDITIVE MANUFACTURING

As part of my Master’s program, I am currently undertaking directed research under the guidance of Dr. Yong Chen. My research addresses the growing demand for efficient thermal management in compact and high-performance semiconductor devices. The focus is on developing micro-scale thermal management solutions to optimize heat dissipation in semiconductor chips using additive manufacturing techniques.

Current Focus I am currently conducting thermal simulations using COMSOL Multiphysics to predict heat transfer characteristics in micro-scale heat sinks. These simulations include: Material-Specific Analysis: Modeling the heat dissipation properties of graphene and CuCrZr alloy, materials known for their compatibility with Electrohydrodynamic Jet Printing (EHD-Jet Printing) and Selective Laser Melting (SLM). Design Optimization: Assessing the impact of key design parameters—such as fin structures, surface area, and material thermal conductivity—to achieve optimal heat sink performance.

Future Plans In the upcoming Spring 2025 semester, I plan to design prototypes by using AUTOCAD to create detailed 3D designs of the heat sinks. If possible, I also aim to - Prototype Fabrication: Fabricate the heat sink prototypes using the most suitable additive manufacturing techniques. Experimental Validation: Where possible, conduct thermal experiments to validate the simulation results and further refine the designs.

I am always open to new ideas, insights, and collaborations that can expand the scope and impact of this research. If you have suggestions or innovative approaches related to micro-scale thermal management, advanced materials, or additive manufacturing techniques, please feel free to reach out. I am excited to explore new possibilities and work together to advance this field!
