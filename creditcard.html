Analysis and Fabrication of Polymer Components

I worked as part of a four-member cross-functional team on the analysis, fabrication, and validation of a hardware component, with emphasis on manufacturability, and part quality. My role focused on understanding how design geometry and material behavior influence fabrication outcomes.

I analyzed four design variants using finite element analysis in ANSYS, applying material properties, defining boundary conditions, allowing comparison of deformation behavior across designs. The simulation results were used to identify a configuration with lower deformation risk and more uniform load distribution, improving robustness prior to fabrication and reducing the likelihood of fabrication defects.

Following design selection, the component was fabricated where process considerations such as geometry complexity, dimensional stability, and fabrication fidelity were evaluated. Post-fabrication, part quality was assessed through dimensional measurements and visual inspection, verifying dimensional accuracy, surface consistency, and overall omponent integrity. Additional custom components were designed and fabricated to ensure compatibility with downstream assembly, while final mechanical integration was handled by mechanical engineers in the team.

Overall, this project strengthened my ability to use simulation as a process decision tool, connect design choices to manufacturing outcomes, and validate part quality through inspection.
